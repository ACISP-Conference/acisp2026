ACISP 2026

Call for Papers

The 31st Australasian Conference on Information Security and Privacy (ACISP 2026) invites submissions of original papers presenting novel research results in all aspects of information security and privacy. This 31st ACISP will take place in Perth. We welcome papers presenting theories, techniques, implementations, applications, and practical experiences on a variety of topics.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Blockchain technology
  • Cryptology
  • Database security
  • Digital forensics
  • Hardware, Side Channels, and Cyber-physical Systems Security
  • Machine Learning and Security
  • Network security
  • Privacy and Anonymity
  • Software security
  • System security
  • Web Security
Systematization of Knowledge (SoK) Papers.

We solicit systematization of knowledge (SoK) papers that evaluate, systematize, and contextualize existing knowledge for topics of broad interest to the cybersecurity community. Examples of SoK papers include papers that provide an important new viewpoint on an established, major research area, support or challenge long-held beliefs in such an area with compelling evidence, or provide an extensive and realistic evaluation and comparison of competing approaches to solving specific security problems.

SoK papers must include the prefix “SoK:” in the title of the paper and will be reviewed by the full PC. They will be accepted based on their treatment of existing work and value to the community.

Instructions for Authors

ACISP 2026 will have two rounds of submission:

Round 1 Deadline:
Paper submission: 27 Nov 2025
Author notification: 15 Jan 2026

Round 2 Deadline:
Paper submission: 26 February 2026
Author notification: 16 April 2026

Camera Ready: 30 April 2026

The first round will have the results, including reject, major revision, and accept. For the major revision, we encourage authors to submit the updated manuscripts to round two. Round two only includes accept and reject.

All submissions must be received by 11:59PM AoE (UTC-12) on the day of the corresponding deadline. Submitted papers must not substantially overlap with papers that have been published or accepted for publication, or that are simultaneously in submission to a journal, conference, or workshop with published proceedings. All submissions should be properly anonymized with no author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, or obvious references. Papers not properly anonymized may be rejected without reviews.

All submitted papers will be evaluated based on their merits, particularly their importance to cryptography and cybersecurity, novelty, quality of execution, and presentation. For papers that might raise ethical concerns, authors are expected to convince reviewers that proper procedures (such as IRB approval or responsible disclosure) have been followed and due diligence has been made to minimize potential harm. Submissions not meeting the submission guidelines risk rejection regardless of their merits.

Special Tracks

In conjunction with the 31st Australasian Conference on Information Security and Privacy (ACISP 2026), ACISP 2026 is pleased to announce a Special Track to be held alongside the main conference. The Special Track aims to provide an additional venue for high-quality research that may not fit the scope or acceptance threshold of the main track, while maintaining the same academic standards and submission requirements as ACISP.

Authors submitting to the main track may be offered the opportunity to publish in the Special Track if their work is deemed valuable but not competitive enough for the main proceedings. All submissions will undergo rigorous peer review by the ACISP Program Committee.

Accepted papers of the Special Track will be included in the new journal Pragmatic Cybersecurity and presented during the Special Track sessions.

Scope and Objectives

The Special Track invites original, unpublished research covering all areas of information security, privacy, cryptography, and cybersecurity systems. It is designed to:

  • Encourage broader participation from academia, industry, and early-career researchers.
  • Support practical and impactful work with real-world relevance.
  • Provide a dedicated forum for emerging PhD researchers to present their work.

The Special Track consists of three sub-tracks:

  1. Industry Track

    This track welcomes submissions that emphasize:

    • Practical deployments, prototypes, and real-world case studies
    • Industrial challenges, system designs, and security evaluations
    • Lessons learned from applied security and privacy solutions
    • Technology transfer, commercialization, and industry partnerships

    Submissions should demonstrate tangible impact, practical insights, or industrial relevance.

  2. (Graduate) Student Symposium

    This symposium is exclusively for submissions authored primarily by (graduate) students. It provides a supportive environment for:

    • Early-stage or ongoing doctoral research
    • Novel ideas, system proposals, theoretical frameworks
    • Constructive feedback and mentoring from senior researchers

    We highly encourage the student submissions. It requires at least one primary author to be enrolled as a (graduate) student candidate at the time of submission.

  3. Vision Track

    This track is exclusively for submissions authored by Cybersecurity Associate Professors or Professors or equivalent from Australian Universities, Research organisations and industry (authors from other regions who meet the criteria are also welcome). The aim is to provide an environment for:

    • Sharing visionary ideas with colleagues
    • Annual meeting for senior Cyber researchers
    • Creating opportunities to build new networks
    • Building collaboration to pursue a new research area or finding like-minded people to apply for funding
    • Exploring the new funding opportunities

    It requires at least one primary author to be a Professor, Associate Professor, or equivalent.

    Submission requirements:

    • One of the authors must be a professor, associate professor, or equivalent who must attend and present the paper, which should be highlighted.
    • The paper can have other authors.
    • The paper will go through light-touch reviews to provide constructive feedback
    • The paper must have an element of a visionary idea

Submission Requirements (Special Track)

The Special Track follows the same submission format, policies, and requirements as the ACISP 2026 main track, including:

  • Paper formatting and page limits
  • Anonymised submissions
  • Originality and dual-submission policies
  • Review criteria and ethical standards

Paper Format

Submissions must be written in English, a PDF file using Springer's LNCS package, no more than 20 pages including bibliography, excluding well-marked appendices, and supplementary material. Author instructions and LaTeX/Word templates for LNCS publications can be found via the following link: https://www.springer.com/gp/computer-science/lncs/conference-proceedings-guidelines. Note that reviewers are not required to read the appendices or any supplementary material. Papers should be sufficiently intelligible and self-contained without appendices so that PC members can make decisions without reading appendices. Authors should not change any style of the LNCS format. Submissions not following the required format may be rejected without review.

The proceedings will be published by Springer-Verlag as a volume of the Lecture Notes in Computer Science series. Authors of accepted papers must guarantee that at least one of the authors will register and attend the conference and present their paper.
